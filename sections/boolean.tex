\section{Boolean}\label{sec:boolean}

The \deff{bool} object abstracts a boolean value, parameterized by
  its \deff{if} attribute---an object of two arguments that returns either
  its first argument (for ``true'') or its second one (for ``false'').
The two objects \deff{true} and \deff{false} are simply two applications
  of \deff{bool}, with opposite implementations of \deff{if}.

Every boolean also has \deff{not}, \deff{and}, and \deff{or} attributes.
